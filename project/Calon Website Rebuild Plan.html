§ 01

Executive summary

Calon’s current site sells a generic AI agency. The real business is an operational systems and automation consultancy for founder-led service businesses. Those are two different companies, and the website is wearing the wrong one.

The live site leads with chatbots, dashboards, invented product names (“PricePilot AI,” “reactivation engine,” “Voice AI receptionist”) and an industry list of eight-plus sectors — law, healthcare, retail, finance, education, manufacturing, food & beverage, real estate. That is the opposite of Calon’s actual commercial direction. The OS explicitly tells us to narrow to three priority sectors (Security/FM/Field Service, Trades/Contractors, Hospitality/Venues), lead with workflow bottlenecks rather than AI capabilities, and standardise the entry offer around an Operating Bottleneck Diagnostic leading into a defined Phase 1 build.

This document gives you a complete rebuild: new positioning, new sitemap, page-by-page structure with section headlines and copy direction, a standardised CTA system, a remove/keep/rewrite list against the current pages, and a three-phase implementation order for today, this week, and next month.

§ 02

Diagnosis of the current site

Pulled from calonaisolutions.com, /industries, /about, /ai-solutions-case-studies…. Eight concrete problems, in order of commercial damage.

1. The site is trying to be relevant to everyone

The current Industries page carries eight sectors — Retail, Healthcare, Finance, Manufacturing, Food & Beverage, Hospitality, Education, Real Estate — plus a header line that lists “law, healthcare, retail, finance, education, and beyond.” That breadth signals generalist. Founder-led service businesses don’t buy from generalists; they buy from specialists who obviously understand their workflow.

2. It leads with AI categories, not operational pain

The homepage opens on “Our AI consultancy, Designed to Help You Succeed, Delivered Anywhere – Your Global Partner in Innovation.” It’s capability talk with no workflow inside it. A prospect reading this cannot tell whether Calon is for a 12-person security firm or a FTSE 250 insurer.

3. It reads as speculative product marketing

Testimonials reference named products (“PricePilot AI,” “reactivation engine,” “Voice AI receptionist,” “e-commerce chatbot”) with very round numbers (17%, 23%, 28%, 35%, 40%). To the target buyer — a sceptical founder — this reads as invented. The OS explicitly says not to present speculative enterprise use cases as the business model.

4. The offer is invisible

The three-lane model (Operational Workspace · Bespoke Workflows · Ongoing Optimisation & Support) is the actual service architecture. It does not appear anywhere on the current site. Without it, the offer looks like “we do AI things.”

5. There is no entry door

No Diagnostic. No Phase 1. No defined first step. The CTA is a generic contact form that treats a serious operational conversation and a vendor spam request identically.

6. CTA logic is noisy

The live pages mix “Let’s Talk,” “Get Started Today,” “Contact Us,” “Book Call,” “Read More,” and “Learn More About [sector] Solutions” with no hierarchy. Nothing is the primary action.

7. Proof is mixed with concept

The case study page describes a chatbot project for a generic “prominent ecommerce company” with no name, no sector detail, no before/after at the workflow level. It reads more like content marketing than proof.

8. Language the OS explicitly says to avoid

“Global partner in innovation.” “AI engines powering businesses in law, healthcare, retail, finance, education, and beyond.” “Integrating AI into your existing environment.” These are generic AI-agency phrases the OS flags directly.

Net effect

The current site attracts the wrong leads (too broad, too speculative), gives them nothing concrete to buy, and funnels them into a generic contact form. It is actively working against the commercial direction defined in the OS.

§ 03

New positioning & messaging hierarchy

This is the master stack. Every page inherits from it. Nothing on the site should contradict it.

Master positioning

Operational systems and automation for founder-led service businesses.

Master subhead

We replace the manual bottlenecks that run your operation — the quote-to-job handover, the scheduling chaos, the admin drag — with systems built around how you actually work. Clearer visibility, tighter control, measurable first-phase change.

Value proposition — in one sentence, three ways

For the homepage
We fix the operational bottleneck that’s costing you time, margin, and control — then we build around it.
For outreach
Founder-led service businesses hit a wall when the workflow depends on one person holding it together. We design the systems that get it off their shoulders.
For sales calls
We don’t sell AI. We sell working operational systems. The first build has to earn its place in your day-to-day, or we’ve failed.

The three lanes

Lane 01 — Control layer

Operational Workspace

One place for visibility, ownership, next steps, reporting. The operating environment the business runs out of, every day.

  • Centralised oversight
  • Workflow visibility
  • Next-step control
  • Reporting integrity
Lane 02 — Execution layer

Bespoke Workflows

Tailored automation built around how you actually operate. Not chatbots bolted on, not a platform to learn — your workflow, encoded.

  • Lead & quote follow-up
  • Quote-to-job handover
  • Scheduling & dispatch
  • Onboarding, admin, reporting
Lane 03 — Compounding layer

Optimisation & Support

The part where most projects fail. Ongoing refinement, training, reporting improvements and system evolution after Phase 1 goes live.

  • Support & training
  • Reporting improvements
  • Continuous refinement
  • System evolution

Phase 1 logic — the commercial engine

Every engagement starts the same way: Operating Bottleneck Diagnostic → Phase 1 build → measurable shift → path into Lane 1 / Lane 3 expansion. Phase 1 removes one clearly defined bottleneck and produces a usable working system. Not a vague transformation. Not a pilot. A system the team uses on Monday.

DiagnosticUnderstand the workflow and find the bottleneck.
MapCurrent system, handovers, dropped balls, friction points.
Phase 1 buildOne bottleneck. One working system. Defined outcome.
LaunchMeasurable before/after. Team trained. Live.
OptimiseLane 1 expansion, Lane 3 support, next bottleneck.

ICP — who the site is written for

Primary: Founder-led service businesses, typically 5–80 staff, with a repeatable service workflow, manual bottlenecks, fragmented tools, and founder dependency. Priority sectors for the next 90 days: Security / FM / Field Service · Trades / Contractors · Hospitality / Venues.

Secondary (optional, handled carefully): Recruitment, Property, Training, Care — only if copy is strong and the fit is genuine.

Not for: Enterprises buying an “AI strategy,” agencies wanting chatbots, anyone shopping for a platform.

Core pain language (site-wide word bank)

Bottleneck · admin drag · dropped balls · handover breakdown · opportunity leakage · fragmented tools · founder dependency · no visibility · the job is done but nobody logged it · the quote went out but nobody chased · the invoice is late because the job sheet is on a van seat.

Proof language

Use bottleneck → build → shift framing. Not percentages without context. Say what broke, what we built, what changed on Monday morning. If we can’t say the client name, say the operating environment (“a 40-vehicle facilities firm,” “a mid-market security contractor,” “a two-site hospitality group”).

Language to favour · language to avoid

Favour

  • Operational systems
  • Bottlenecks, handovers, drop-offs
  • Visibility, ownership, control
  • Quote-to-job, scheduling, admin
  • Founder dependency
  • Practical implementation
  • Phase 1 build
  • Measurable operational shift
  • Centralised workspace

Avoid

  • Next-gen / cutting-edge AI
  • AI-first transformation
  • Intelligent automation platform
  • We automate everything
  • End-to-end AI solutions
  • Global partner in innovation
  • “Powering businesses in [8 sectors]”
  • Named product marketing (PricePilot AI, etc.)
  • Generic “dashboard” / “engine” language

§ 07

Voice & language system

The site should read like a senior operator who has done the work, not a vendor selling the category.

Voice principles

  1. Specific before clever. “The job sheet is on the van seat” beats “we unlock operational excellence.”
  2. Operational, not aspirational. Describe Monday mornings, not futures.
  3. Confident refusal. What Calon won’t do is a feature, not a disclaimer.
  4. Short sentences for claims. Longer sentences for context. Rhythm matters.
  5. No emoji. No exclamation marks. No “🚀 transformations.” Calon is read by founders, not skimmed by TikTok.
  6. British English throughout. Optimisation, organisation, recognise.

Headline pattern library

Before (current site)

  • “Our AI consultancy, Designed to Help You Succeed, Delivered Anywhere – Your Global Partner in Innovation.”
  • “We design AI that evolves with your business.”
  • “We’re the brains behind AI engines powering businesses in law, healthcare, retail, finance, education, and beyond.”
  • “Growth isn’t a tech problem.”

After

  • “Operational systems for founder-led service businesses.”
  • “We fix the bottleneck that’s costing you time, margin and control — then we build around it.”
  • “Workflow first. One bottleneck at a time. A working system by launch.”
  • “The problem isn’t your tech. It’s the handover between sales and ops.”

Banned phrases

“Global partner,” “end-to-end AI,” “AI-first,” “intelligent automation platform,” “powering businesses in [5+ sectors],” “designed to help you succeed,” “cutting-edge,” “we automate everything,” “transformation journey,” named fake products (“PricePilot,” “Reactivation Engine,” etc.).

Implementation priority

Three waves. The aim is to stop the site actively hurting the business within 24 hours, then rebuild over 4–6 weeks.

Today — stop the bleeding

  1. Unpublish the /industries hub and the four least-aligned sector sub-pages (Retail, Finance, Manufacturing, Food&Bev, Real Estate, Education). Hold only Hospitality + anything genuinely sold.
  2. Remove the invented-product testimonial carousel from the homepage.
  3. Replace the hero headline with: Operational systems for founder-led service businesses. Plain HTML swap, not a redesign.
  4. Replace all CTAs with Book the Diagnostic pointing to a Calendly link.
  5. Unpublish /ai-solutions-case-studies… and /boost-your-business-with-a-gpt-assistant.

Weeks 1–2 — structural rebuild

  1. Write and ship the new Homepage per §05.
  2. Build /how-we-work. This is the second-most-important page on the site.
  3. Build /diagnostic.
  4. Build /sectors/security-fm with full sector template. Use as reference for the other two.
  5. Stand up /proof with 2 example builds, even if placeholders for numbers — bottleneck/build/shift framing from day one.

Weeks 3–6 — fill out & compound

  1. Build /sectors/trades and /sectors/hospitality.
  2. Rewrite /about in the new voice.
  3. Launch /insights with three foundational posts (“What a good Phase 1 looks like,” “Why most automation projects fail,” “The real cost of founder dependency”).
  4. Add a third proof piece. Begin treating proof as a delivery output on every engagement.
  5. Commission simple photography or operational imagery — no stock AI brains, no robotic hands.
§ 10

Notes for the designer

We’re not briefing design yet, but the plan assumes a visual system that reinforces the positioning. Guardrails for whoever picks this up next:

  • Editorial, not agency-flashy. Stronger hierarchy, calmer palette, more white space, less motion.
  • Serif for positioning, sans for operational copy. A crisp serif (similar feel to what this document uses) carries the brand voice; a clean sans carries the work.
  • No stock AI imagery. No brain graphics, no glowing circuits, no robotic hands. Either real operational photography or deliberate typographic layouts.
  • Proof blocks are a designed component. They’re used on the homepage, the proof page, and sector pages — treat them as a first-class module.
  • CTA consistency is visual, not just verbal. One button style, one colour, one place.
  • Sector pages share a template. Design the template once; change only the sector-specific opening scene.
  • The Diagnostic page is the conversion moment. Treat it like the product page on a DTC site. It earns its own layout.

What happens next

Approve or redline this plan. Once positioning, sitemap and priority are signed off, we move to visual direction and page-level design — starting with the homepage and /how-we-work, then the sector template, then /diagnostic.
